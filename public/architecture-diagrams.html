01 · Structural Diagram

Class Diagram of the Three Contracts

The architecture separates business logic (PalindromePay), deterministic wallet creation (factory, CREATE2), and custody of funds (one wallet per deal). The wallet does not blindly trust the escrow contract: it reads the deal state back and requires its own outcome-bound EIP-712 signatures from 2 of the 3 participants at withdrawal time.

    PalindromePay "1" o-- "n" EscrowDeal : manages
    EscrowDeal --> State : state
    PalindromePay ..> Role : events
    PalindromePay --> PalindromePayWalletFactory : WALLET_FACTORY
    PalindromePayWalletFactory ..> PalindromePayWallet : CREATE2 deploy per deal
    PalindromePayWallet --> PalindromePay : reads getEscrow(), FEE_RECEIVER()

The signatures in EscrowDeal are only collected by the escrow contract — they are cryptographically re-verified in the wallet during withdraw(). Each signature binds to exactly one outcome via the PayoutAuthorization typehash (COMPLETE = 3, REFUNDED = 4, CANCELED = 5).

02 · Behavioral Diagram

State Diagram of the Escrow Lifecycle

Six states, three of them terminal. Terminal states release the funds — who receives them is determined by the state itself: COMPLETE pays the seller (minus the 1% platform fee, plus the arbiter fee if an arbiter resolved the dispute), REFUNDED and CANCELED refund the buyer in full.

%%{init: {'theme':'neutral'}}%%
stateDiagram-v2
    [*] --> AWAITING_PAYMENT : createEscrow()
    [*] --> AWAITING_DELIVERY : createEscrowAndDeposit()

    AWAITING_PAYMENT --> AWAITING_DELIVERY : deposit() — buyer
    AWAITING_PAYMENT --> CANCELED : requestCancel() both parties / timeout

    AWAITING_DELIVERY --> COMPLETE : confirmDelivery(buyerSig) — buyer
    AWAITING_DELIVERY --> COMPLETE : autoRelease() — seller, after maturityTime,\nseller sig present, no dispute/cancel
    AWAITING_DELIVERY --> CANCELED : requestCancel() both parties /\ncancelByTimeout()
    AWAITING_DELIVERY --> DISPUTED : startDispute() — buyer or seller

    DISPUTED --> COMPLETE : submitArbiterDecision(COMPLETE)\nevidence from both sides or 30 d + 1 h
    DISPUTED --> REFUNDED : submitArbiterDecision(REFUNDED)
    DISPUTED --> REFUNDED : refundAfterDisputeTimeout()\nbuyer, after 30 d + 1 h without a decision

    COMPLETE --> [*] : withdraw() → seller − 1% fee (± arbiter fee)
    REFUNDED --> [*] : withdraw() → buyer (full)
    CANCELED --> [*] : withdraw() → buyer (full)

The arbiter may only decide once both parties have submitted evidence (disputeStatus bitmap) — or alternatively after DISPUTE_LONG_TIMEOUT (30 days) + TIMEOUT_BUFFER (1 hour) has elapsed. If the arbiter never decides, the buyer can self-refund after the same deadline.

03 · Activity Diagram

Escrow Flow: Happy Path and Dispute Path

The normal case works without the arbiter — they only become active in a dispute (and only then does their fee apply). The wallet address is deterministically known via CREATE2 even before the deposit.

%%{init: {'theme':'neutral'}}%%
flowchart TD
    A([Buyer/seller agree on a deal]) --> B[createEscrow or createEscrowAndDeposit\ntoken, amount, deadline, optional arbiter + fee]
    B --> C[Wallet address derived\ndeterministically via CREATE2]
    C --> D[Buyer: deposit\ntokens flow into the deal wallet]
    D --> E[Seller: acceptEscrow\nsigns outcome COMPLETE]
    E --> F{Delivery /\nservice acceptable?}

    F -- yes --> G[Buyer: confirmDelivery\nsigns outcome COMPLETE]
    G --> H[[State → COMPLETE]]

    F -- deadline passed,\nbuyer unresponsive --> I[Seller: autoRelease\nafter maturityTime]
    I --> H

    F -- no --> J[startDispute\nbuyer or seller]
    J --> K[Both parties: submitDisputeMessage\nevidence as IPFS hash]
    K --> L{Arbiter decides\nwithin 30 days?}
    L -- yes --> M[submitArbiterDecision\nsigns the exact ruling]
    M --> N{Ruling}
    N -- for seller --> H
    N -- for buyer --> O[[State → REFUNDED]]
    L -- no --> P[Buyer: refundAfterDisputeTimeout\nafter 30 d + 1 h]
    P --> O

    F -- both want out --> Q[requestCancel by buyer and seller]
    Q --> R[[State → CANCELED]]

    H --> S[withdraw on the wallet\n2-of-3 signature check]
    O --> S
    R --> S
    S --> T([Payout: COMPLETE → seller − fees,\notherwise → buyer in full])

04 · Sequence Diagram

Outcome-Bound 2-of-3 Payout

The core of the security model: no single participant — not even the escrow contract itself — can move funds. During withdraw() the wallet independently verifies that at least two participants signed exactly the outcome that occurred.

%%{init: {'theme':'neutral'}}%%
sequenceDiagram
    autonumber
    actor B as Buyer
    actor S as Seller
    participant E as PalindromePay
    participant W as Deal wallet
    participant T as ERC20 token

    B->>E: deposit(escrowId)
    E->>T: transferFrom(buyer → wallet)
    Note over E: State = AWAITING_DELIVERY

    S->>E: acceptEscrow(sellerSig)
    Note right of E: EIP-712 sig, outcome = COMPLETE,\nstored in the deal

    B->>E: confirmDelivery(buyerSig)
    Note right of E: EIP-712 sig, outcome = COMPLETE
    E->>E: _setState(COMPLETE), payout proposed

    S->>W: withdraw()
    W->>E: getEscrow(ESCROW_ID)
    E-->>W: EscrowDeal (state, participants, sigs)
    W->>W: Verify ≥ 2 valid sigs\nfor outcome COMPLETE
    W->>T: transfer(1% → FEE_RECEIVER)
    W->>T: transfer(remainder → seller)
    Note over W: withdrawn = true,\nwallet must be empty

In the dispute case the arbiter provides the second signature instead — submitArbiterDecision binds it to exactly the ruling made. A COMPLETE signature can never authorize a REFUNDED payout and vice versa; replay is prevented via usedSignatures and nonce bitmaps.
